Plan Your Float

Everything in one place — the route, parking, getting back to your car, and the details that trip people up.

The Route: Barber Park → Ann Morrison Park

Put in at Barber Park and float about 6 miles through town to the take-out at Ann Morrison Park — 2 to 3 hours on the water.

Season

Mid-June to Labor Day, conditions permitting

Park gates

8 a.m. to sunset

Distance

~6 miles

Time on water

2–3 hours

Parking at Barber Park

  • •$7 per day (plus a 50¢ fee if paying by card).
  • •Kiosk hours: 10 a.m.–7:30 p.m. weekdays, from 9:30 a.m. weekends & holidays.
  • •No re-entry without paying again — use the drop-off area when you return for gear or people.
  • •The lot closes to entry at 7:30 p.m., but you can still drive out until sunset. Park only in marked spots.

Getting Back to Your Car

  • •Shuttle bus: a paid shuttle runs between Ann Morrison and Barber Park all season. Check current hours and pricing before you go.
  • •Two cars: leave one at Ann Morrison, then drive to the put-in and skip the shuttle.

Renting vs. Bringing Your Own

  • •Rafts, tubes, and kayaks rent on-site at Barber Park during the season. Life vests are included with raft rentals and available on request.
  • •Bringing your own tube? Arrive with it already inflated — the air stations at Barber Park have been removed.
  • •No glass containers in the parks or on the river.

Tips for Your Crew

What you need to know depends on who's coming along.

First Time?

  • •Rent at Barber Park — gear, life vests, and the shuttle back are all handled for you.
  • •Give yourself a full afternoon: 2–3 hours floating plus parking, setup, and the ride back.
  • •Wear covered shoes that can get wet — the river bottom is rocky.
  • •The water is chilly even on hot days. If you get cold, just get off the water.

Floating with Kids?

  • •Idaho law requires kids 14 and under to wear a life jacket on rafts, kayaks, and canoes.
  • •Ada County advises against bringing infants or small children under 50 lbs on the river.
  • •Make sure everyone in your group can swim, and use the buddy system.
  • •A raft is steadier than tubes for young kids — and rentals include life vests.

Floated Before?

  • •Check the flow above before you head out — conditions change through the season.
  • •Arrive with tubes already inflated — the Barber Park air stations have been removed.
  • •Remember: no re-entry to the fee lot without paying again. Use the drop-off area.
  • •Leave the river cleaner than you found it.

Safety First

The Boise River is generally calm, but conditions can change. A few basics make a real difference.

  • Wear a properly fitted PFD — required by Idaho law for children 14 and under on rafts, kayaks, and canoes.
  • Make sure everyone in your party can swim, use the buddy system, and tell someone your plan.
  • Stay aware of strainers, low branches, and changing conditions. Note a landmark so you can describe your location if you need help.
  • If you fall in: stay calm, feet downstream, use your arms to ferry across the current, and let your PFD do its job.
  • Alcohol, drugs, and the river don't mix. No glass in the parks or on the water.

Common Questions

Quick answers to the things floaters ask most.

When is float season?

Typically mid-June through Labor Day, seven days a week — but opening and closing dates depend on weather and water conditions. Always confirm the season is open at floattheboise.org before you go.

How long does the float take?

About 2–3 hours on the water for the ~6-mile run from Barber Park to Ann Morrison Park, depending on flow. Budget a full afternoon once you add parking, setup, and the shuttle back.

How much is parking?

$7 per day at Barber Park (plus a 50¢ fee for card payments). There's no re-entry to the fee lot without paying again, so use the drop-off area when you come back for gear or people after your float.

How do I get back to my car?

A paid shuttle bus runs between Ann Morrison Park and Barber Park during the float season. Groups with two cars often leave one near Ann Morrison before driving to the put-in. Check current shuttle details before you go.

Can I bring my own tube?

Yes — but arrive with it already inflated. The air stations at Barber Park have been removed. Heavy-duty river tubes hold up much better than pool floats on this rocky section.

Can kids float?

Yes, with precautions. Idaho law requires children 14 and under to wear an approved life jacket on rafts, kayaks, and canoes, and Ada County advises against bringing infants or small children under 50 lbs on the river. Everyone in your group should know how to swim.

What's not allowed?

Glass containers are prohibited in the parks and on the river. Ada County's guidance is simple: alcohol, drugs, and the river don't mix. Pack out everything you bring — leave the river cleaner than you found it.

Is the water cold?

Yes — the river runs cool all summer. That's most of the appeal on a 95° day, but it also means you should get off the water if you start feeling cold, especially with kids along.
